# Interview: Mariana Castilho

**Role:** Product designer at poolside, previously at Vercel. Creator of uilabs.dev.

Mariana is a Brazilian product designer who created uilabs.dev where she shares her UI explorations. In this interview they discuss product design, how she learned to code as a designer, and how that influenced her design process.

## Key Topics

### Background

- Studied advertising/marketing in school while also studying design. Eventually focused on marketing degree per parents' advice.
- Her relationship with design started early - grew up on internet forums, was into Dragon Ball, web design, video games, and art. Learned interface/software design on her own.

### Learning to code as a designer

- Learning to code changed how she approaches design. Understanding implementation constraints makes her a better designer.
- Side projects and free explorations are essential for polishing your craft.

### Passion and ambition

- She's passionate about creating extraordinary things. There's no such thing as perfection, but she always tries to improve.
- Knowing your limits and allowing yourself to rest and recharge is important. Time management and planning how much energy to invest helps a lot.

### On UI explorations (uilabs.dev)

- Created uilabs.dev as a place to share UI experiments and explorations.
- Side projects give freedom to experiment without production constraints.
