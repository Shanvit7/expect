# Interview: Henry Heffernan

**Role:** Design engineer at Vercel, working on v0.

Henry is a design engineer working at Vercel. He contributed to a lot of Vercel's new marketing pages, Next.js Conf, and more. He also has an impressive personal site. In this interview, they discuss animations, easings, design engineering, and how he got his job at Vercel.

## Key Topics

### Background and career path

- Henry built an impressive personal site during his last year of college after struggling to get job offers as an unremarkable graduate on paper. The site went viral on Twitter and led to Guillermo (Vercel's CEO) reaching out via DMs.
- He started on Vercel's marketing team before moving to the design engineering team, where he worked on projects like the Vercel homepage redesign.

### On developing taste

- Taste is difficult to define and isn't inherently a design engineering trait - it's more of a designer trait that design engineers hold.
- To develop taste: develop your design muscles first, break the problem down. Look at great work, study it, and try to understand why it works.

### Animation tools at Vercel

- At Vercel, they lean towards native CSS animations as much as possible because of performance focus. Framer Motion is JavaScript and requires hydration before animations can play.
- If doing layout animations, Framer Motion is basically required. But most marketing page animations are basic enough to fake with transforms.

### When to animate

- Use taste to dictate when to animate. On marketing pages, you're trying to tell a story.
- Think about what the hero moment is and what supporting elements help tell that story. Animations should guide users' attention.

### Advice for aspiring design engineers

- Build stuff. The more you build, the better you get.
- If you have passion for it and keep flexing your design and engineering muscles, you'll improve over time.
