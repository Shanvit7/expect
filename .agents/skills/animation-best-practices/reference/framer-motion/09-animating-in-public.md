# Animating in public

I got my first job at an American start up through Twitter, because I shared my work there.

Guillermo Rauch reached out to me on Twitter as well, and that's how I got my job at Vercel.

If I wasn't posting my work on Twitter, you probably wouldn't be taking this course right now, as you wouldn't even know I exist.

What I'm trying to say here is that building in public is incredibly powerful. If you don't have a job yet, it'll increase your chances to get one and if you have a job already, it'll strengthen your position on the market, or, if you want to start your own thing, you'll already have an audience.

## What does this have to do with animations?

This is a homework lesson. I'd want you to craft an animation based on the things we've learned, and share it on Twitter or send it privately. You can tag me in the tweet and we can have a nice interaction there.

If you would want me to review the animation first, you can share it on the course's Discord server and I'll give you feedback.
